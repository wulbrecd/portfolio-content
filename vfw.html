What is the VFW?

One major organization for Veterans is the VFW (the Veterans of Foreign Wars). Originally tracing it's roots back to 1899, in 1914, a bunch of smaller groups came toghether and, in 1936, officially formed the VFW. While primarily an organization for those Veterans who have been in some sort of war or conflict, they are a solid community of Veterans. Lately, they have relaxed the requirements to join from having been in war or conflict to having served during a time of war in conflict in general in an attempt to recruit a younger audience to keep the organization going.

There are many VFW posts across the country. While you may not be a member, they allow all Veterans and Dependants to visit their posts. As another Veteran organization around the country, they allow partnerships with any other Veteran organization which include utilizing their post, providing resources to Veterans, and fundraising assistance. Other programs provided from the VFW are financial aid and VA resource connections.
